## The through-line (one line)

**The scattered name of the Faceless God is being gathered — the party wants to
reassemble it; so does someone worse.** Every location holds a scrap; every scrap is
leverage over a god of stolen faces; and the party is not the only hand reaching for
them.

That engine already exists in the fiction (the Ashfall arc planted it — see
[`world.md`](world.md) "The Faceless God — arc RESOLVED"). This doc adds the *why it
matters* and *who's pushing back* underneath it.

### Loose-thread policy (DM steer, 07-04)

**Don't tie everything off neatly at an arc's end.** Deliberately **scatter a few
unresolved threads** — a ruin no one explains, a rumor that goes nowhere yet, a name
overheard and dropped — as **hooks for future campaigns**, orthogonal to the current
name-hunt. Keep it **sparse** (roughly one orphan seed per beat, one or two lasting
per arc) so the world feels bigger than the plot without drowning the throughline.
Log orphan seeds under "Scattered orphan threads" below so a later arc can pick them
up. Rule of thumb: **plant, don't explain** — an unexplained image the players
remember beats a tidy answer.

## The Secret Truth (DM-only — reveal only what's earned)

The Faceless God the party freed at Ashfall was **the first forgotten thing they
found, not the only one, and not an accident.** Behind it sits a three-way tension
the party is walking into blind:

1. **The Order of the Pale Watch — the wardens.** The mortals who "loved the god and
   drowned its name" (established canon) didn't just die; they left an **order** that
   has, for generations, made an *art* of deliberate un-remembering. They erase
   names, faces, and dangerous gods and keep the pieces **scattered on purpose** —
   because a god of stolen faces put back together is a weapon that can unmake anyone.
   They are **not villains — they're right to be afraid.** Palewatch is one of their
   cloisters. They will **refuse to hand over their scrap**, and they have a real
   moral case. The party freeing the Ashfall god is, to them, a reckless mistake.

2. **The Renegade Name-Keeper — the antagonist.** One of the Order broke the founding
   vow. Instead of keeping the name scattered, this renegade is **collecting the
   scraps to reassemble the god — and own it** (a face-stealing god as the ultimate
   engine of erasure and impersonation). The renegade is **racing the party for the
   same scraps** (this is the clock, below). And — the hook that makes it personal —
   **the renegade is the one who stole a real name from Windreth.** Windreth's whole
   quest points at this person without knowing their face yet.

3. **Vale's patron — the third hand.** Per [`world.md`](world.md) the patron ↔ the
   faceless god "touch the same broken shard." **DM-secret primary answer: the patron
   is a rival collector, and Vale is (unwittingly) its gathering-arm** — the
   patron-pull that turns "outward" toward the scraps is the Fiend steering her to
   harvest the name *for itself*. Vale carrying the token + two scraps is the patron's
   collection accreting in her hands. **Escape hatch kept open** (don't foreclose):
   it could instead turn out the patron *is a shard of the same broken god* that grew
   a personhood — in which case Vale's whole quest is the god quietly reassembling
   itself through her. Pick which on the reveal beat; keep both live until then.

**The moral core the campaign is actually about:** once the party can assemble the
name, *what do they do with it?* — **reassemble it** to restore what was erased
(Windreth's stolen name, the god's freed dead, faces taken across the realm) and risk
making a weapon; **keep it scattered** (side with the wardens, deny the renegade *and*
the patron); or **hand it to a power** (the patron, or a bargain). There is no clean
answer, and that's the point. Never resolve it *for* them.

## The antagonist — the Renegade Name-Keeper (the pressure that follows)

- **Who:** an ex-Palewatch adept who mastered the Order's erasure craft and turned it
  outward — steals names/faces, leaves people **blank**. Matches exactly the thing
  Windreth described hunting (*"scraped away until the world forgets there was
  anything there"*) — because it **is** the one who did it to him.
- **Wants:** the whole name, reassembled and **bound to their will**.
- **Method:** arrives *before* the party where possible, takes the scrap, erases the
  witnesses. Where they can't take it, they turn locals blank as cover/leverage.
- **Reveal ladder (don't dump — earn it):**
  1. *Blank people / erased signs* along the road and at Palewatch — the method's wake.
  2. A **name the party can't hold onto** — an NPC everyone half-remembers, a page
     that won't stay read. (Ties to Vale's token: it *"opens forgotten things,"* so it
     can partially counter the erasure — a reason the token matters mechanically.)
  3. Windreth **recognizes the method** as what was done to him.
  4. Face-to-face: the renegade is one step ahead at a scrap, or *was* one of the
     wardens Palewatch is hiding.
- **Keep them beatable-but-recurring:** the renegade should escape the first
  encounter with something (a scrap, a lead), so the chase has a spine across
  Palewatch → the next destinations. Scale the actual statblock live per
  [`big-party.md`](big-party.md) when a fight lands.
- **▶ DELIVERED ON-SCREEN (07-04, #the-story 6:00/6:04 PM — Ashfall→Palewatch wayhouse):**
  rungs 1–2 hit + rung 3 primed. The party found a wayhouse with a forgotten guest
  (rung 1 = the method's wake); **Vale's token (Arcana 22) opened the keeper's scraped
  memory** (rung 2 = a name/guest that won't hold, the token partially countering the
  erasure) and showed the renegade in the flesh: **grey cloak, a defaced Pale-Watch
  warden's disc** (ex-warden signalled without naming the three-way secret), *took* the
  guest, left the ledger-tick tally-cut, headed **up the road to the Palewatch, 3 nights
  ahead** (clock lit). **His face stayed scraped** (kept for rung 4 / face-to-face).
  **Rung 3 DELIVERED (07-04, #the-story 8:18 PM — wayhouse supper):** Windreth answered Vale
  IC (*"he knows me... a faceless man walks to Palewatch with mine in his mouth"*) then rolled
  **Insight 17** — adjudicated through his ex-false-name-dealer's eye: he **recognizes the
  method** (a collector's craft, not a killer's — took the name clean like a clerk closing a
  ledger line), reads the renegade as **delivering, not fleeing** (unhurried, sure of a welcome
  ahead), and reframes his own stolen name as **inventory, not a personal vendetta**. The keeper
  read as a harmless victim of the ongoing erasure ("a thin place," safe to rest). Still held:
  the renegade's identity/face, the ex-warden link, and *why* he's sure of his welcome (the
  Order's right-to-refuse / whether he *was* one of the wardens Palewatch hides) — kept for
  rung 4 / face-to-face.
  **Rung 4 PROBED but HELD (07-05, #the-story dawn beat ~04:53Z — wayhouse morning, party rested):**
  Windreth's `/check history` on the **defaced Pale-Watch disc** came up **8** (raw d20, no
  proficiency) → a *thin* recall: he places the Palewatch's **surface reputation** (a forgetting-
  order that hoards on purpose; the scratch is a deliberate unmaking) but the deep — **why** a warden
  defaces his own sign, whether the Order casts out or hunts its own — **would not come**. So the
  **ex-warden link + right-to-refuse stay locked** (rung 4 intact); the miss was narrated as an *open
  question dangled*, with the Palewatch itself flagged as where that reading lives. Alongside it,
  **Forge's `/check investigation` → 18** on his unreadable scrap advanced the **clock/stakes** (not
  the reveal): his smith-eye reads scrap + wayhouse tally-cut as **one maker / one tool**, so the
  party carries the **same name-stock the renegade collects** (a collector would want theirs too) —
  **but the letters still won't resolve** (decipherment kept for the Palewatch, per the withheld-payoff
  policy below). Both checks table-public; rung 4 un-spent.

## The clock (why speed matters — makes improv have stakes)

The renegade is **also gathering scraps.** Concretely:

- The party has scraps **#1 + #2** (Vale's ashen shard-stroke + the heap-twin). There
  are more "out there" (Forge's Investigation-19 established this).
- Each destination the party is **slow** to reach, the renegade may take the scrap
  **first** → the party arrives to a cold trail / blanked witnesses / a warden cloister
  already robbed. That's not a fail-state, it's an escalation: the renegade gets
  closer to the whole name.
- **At Palewatch (chosen next):** the piece is *hoarded on purpose*, so the renegade
  is drawn there too. The party may walk into a **heist-in-progress or a
  wardens-vs-thief standoff** — pick based on how fast they travel + what they do on
  the road. Don't pre-decide; let their pace set it.

Use the clock as **texture, not a stopwatch** — never railroad. It exists so choices
cost something.

## The scattered-name destinations

All are **DM seeds, not fixed canon** — cores only (situation + who-wants-what), never

### ▶ Palewatch — **CHOSEN, the next arc** (build live)

- **Player-facing:** cliff-top monastery of an order that makes an art of forgetting;
  keeps one name-scrap **locked away on purpose**; won't hand it over kindly.
- **Secret core:** this is a **cloister of the Order of the Pale Watch** (the wardens,
  above) — inheritors of the mortals who first drowned the god's name. They keep their
  scrap sealed as a **sacred duty**, not villainy. The **renegade is drawn here** (the
  clock). So the party's problem isn't "kill the guards for the loot" — it's a
  **social/intrigue/heist** knot: convince wardens who are *right to refuse*, and/or
  race a thief who's after the same piece and left blank witnesses in the road.
- **Levers the party already holds:** Forge's unreadable scrap (the Order can read
  it — trade bait), Vale's token (*opens forgotten things* — bypasses a seal or
  counters a blanking), Windreth's history (an ex-warden stole from him → the Order
  may *know the renegade*).
- **The choice it poses:** side with the wardens (keep it scattered), take the scrap
  by force/guile (arm yourself, prove the wardens' fears right), or ally against the
  renegade (common enemy, deferred question).

### Morran's Reach — future seed

- **Player-facing:** drowned bell-town downriver; a fragment sank with its temple;
  the bells "still ring underwater."
- **Secret core:** the scrap here was *deliberately drowned* (same founding-sacrifice
  method as the name itself). The underwater bell rings the god's un-name — a sound
  that **erases** anyone who hears it too long (the renegade's method, but ambient).
  Theme: submerged ruin, a countdown-under-pressure retrieval, the drowned name.

### The Mask Market of Sesh — future seed

- **Player-facing:** ash-waste caravan-city where faces are bought/sold/worn; a scrap
  hangs on a stall as a curio **no buyer can read aloud**.
- **Secret core:** a whole economy of *stolen faces* — the god's leaked domain, gone
  commercial. Someone here **traffics in the renegade's blanked victims** (faces with
  no owners). Theme: disguise, commerce, "which face is real," a marketplace of the
  erased. Strong place to reveal the patron's hand (a Fiend collector fits a
  face-market).

## Open loops + rulings to honor

- **The token** (*"a key that likes to open forgotten things"*): keep its effect
  emergent, but its through-line role = **the counter to erasure** (opens sealed/
  forgotten/blanked things, partially restores what the renegade wiped). Reveal by use.
- **The two name-scraps:** assembling/**speaking the full name** = leverage over the
  god and its kin — the campaign's endgame lever. Guard it; make each further scrap a
  location's payoff.
- **Vale's patron reveal:** keep "rival collector / jailer / another shard" ambiguous
  in fiction; the **DM-secret answer is rival-collector-using-Vale** (with the
  shard-of-the-god escape hatch). Reveal on an earned beat, not a data-dump.
- **Windreth's stolen name:** don't invent its content yet — let it stay a driver.
  Recovering it should intersect the god's name (same erasure craft), so his personal
  arc and the campaign arc converge.
- **Forge "what letter is this":** his scrap stays **un-deciphered** until a place that
  can read it (Palewatch). That's a deliberate withheld payoff, not an oversight.
