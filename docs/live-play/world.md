# World — Ashfall Waystation (setting, lore, rulings, NPCs)

> The durable fiction. Load this when **narrating** or seeding a hook — not needed
> for pure mechanics. Live "where are we right now" lives in
> [`game-state.md`](game-state.md); this file is the world that doesn't change turn
> to turn.

## The frame

**Ashfall Waystation.** A remote stone waystation on the edge of a grey moor as an
ash-coloured dusk settles. The hearth is cold, the keeper gone, the cellar door
scored with deep fresh scratches **from the inside**.

Sandbox tone: players can investigate, search, talk to whatever they find, leave,
or force the cellar — which is where the fights wait if they want them. **Tailor
the hook to each PC** (a cleric senses wrongness; a rogue spots the pried lock; a
warlock feels their patron's pull; a barbarian smells blood). With a bigger party,
seed a *per-PC* sensory beat so each player gets a thread on arrival — see
[`big-party.md`](big-party.md) "Spotlight."

## The threat — living wretches (DM ruling)

The cellar disgorged a hollowed-out thing that was a **person** once (the keeper,
maybe). Up close, after the kill: a face, gone wrong.

- **DM RULING:** these are **LIVING wretches (Humanoid)**, *not* undead — even
  though the engine's stat block is labelled **"Ghoul."** Ignore the type tag.
- **Why it matters:** the living-Humanoid ruling makes *hold person* (and other
  Humanoid-only effects) valid against them. It already paid off — Vale's hold
  person paralysed the first wretch and Forge auto-crit it to death.
- **Narration mask:** never say "paralyzed" / "Ghoul" / numbers to players. The
  thing "seizes up," "locks rigid," "comes apart at the shoulder." See the
  secret-info rule in [`dm-rules.md`](dm-rules.md).

## The cellar

The cellar mouth gapes in the SW corner of the common room (a 2×2 pit on the map),
its door clawed to splinters **from the inside**. The dread points downward. Below
is the **brood** — more wretches in the dark. The descent fight is pre-built:
[`encounters/cellar-brood.md`](encounters/cellar-brood.md).

## NPCs & threads (seeds — DM's call whether to use)

- **The keeper** — missing; likely the first wretch, or the one who let it out.
  A body, a journal, a ledger, or a key could be on the corpse or in the room.
- **What scored the door from inside** — something desperate; the brood, or
  something the brood fears.
- **Loot / clue hooks:** the keeper's body and the common room may hold a key, a
  journal, or claw-scored boards pointing at what's below. Seed if you want the
  party to have a reason to descend beyond curiosity.

## Status of the opening beat

The "Ashfall Waystation" opening was posted to #the-story (tailored to Vale) and
the party has already cleared the upstairs wretch. The world is now **post-first-
fight**: the corpse cools in the common room, the cellar still gapes. New PCs
joining should be folded in as **fellow travelers who reached the waystation** (or
were already inside) — narrate their arrival, not their choices.
